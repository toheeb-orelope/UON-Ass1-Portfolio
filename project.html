Software Engineering Project



During my first year and first semester at the University of Northampton studying Software Engineering, one of the modules focused on imparting the significance of software engineering in real-world scenarios. This encompassed various stages such as problem identification, eliciting characteristics of the problem domain, specifying requirements, conducting system analysis, designing systems, building and testing them, evaluating their performance, implementing them, and ensuring their maintenance. These components constitute the fundamental aspects of software engineering.

Under the guidance of exceptional lecturers, we were divided into subgroups and tasked with a project aimed at addressing the decline in annual revenue at a zoo, attributed to outdated technology. As the leader of the web development team, I collaborated closely with my team leader. Despite our team's commendable performance, we unfortunately lost the contract due to our higher pricing. Nonetheless, I take immense pride in our team's achievements and the high praise we received from our lecturer, resulting in an A+ grade for my exemplary work ethic and performance.

I cannot overstate the incredible support provided by our lecturer, both in terms of delivering lectures and offering additional assistance outside the classroom, ensuring that all students were well-equipped for success.
